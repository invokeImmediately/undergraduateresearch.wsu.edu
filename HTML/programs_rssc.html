Research Skills Short Course (RSSC)

This course is designed for transfer students and high-achieving, rising sophomores already at WSU who are heading into science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) majors and careers. Experienced undergraduate researchers will lead the classes associated with this course, acting as mentors and leaders.

Signing Up for the Course

Applications Will Open Soon

RSSC online applications for the August 2016 course will be available in early February. Stay tuned!

Unique Opportunities

Transfer and current WSU students selected for RSSC participation will be on the fast-track to:

  • securing mentored research positions with WSU faculty who are world-class researchers in their fields
  • receiving one-to-one mentoring and guidance from Undergraduate Research Fellows at the August RSSC session and throughout the fall and spring semesters
  • learning at least five research-oriented skills during the short course
  • becoming a WSU student who adds value to their education by engaging in high-impact and hands-on learning that goes beyond the traditional classroom setting

Eligibility for the Course

RSSC is specially designed for students intending to major in a STEM discipline who are:

  • transferring to WSU from community colleges and other universities
  • high-achieving underclasssmen already at WSU
  • in the first generation of their family to attend college
  • interested in being a valuable and contributing member of a research team
  • ready to take ownership of their education and career

How to Apply

  • Students must complete an online application to apply for RSSC.
  • Submit a copy of your transcripts with your application even if you have submitted them to WSU for admission. (The reason for this requirement is that the Office of Undergraduate Research does not have access to information submitted to for WSU admission purposes.) Note that the transcript you submit for RSSC consideration does not have to be an official copy.
  • Send your transcripts to UGresearch@wsu.edu, or mail them to P.O. Box 644519, Pullman, WA 99164-4519

Information About the Course

What You will Learn in the RSSC

The RSSC is designed to give you the knowledge you will need to begin a successful undergraduate research career at WSU. Among the topics your peer mentors will address are:

  • How to go about connecting with professors whom you can work with on projects that are valuable
  • Improving your communication skills, both written and oral
  • Understanding the literature produced by researchers
  • Developing your record-keeping skills, such as techniques for maintaining an effective laboratory notebook
  • Sharing your research and results with others, such as through poster sessions
