2015 Summer Undergraduate Researcher Poster Symposium

The finale of the summer-long research programs for undergraduates at WSU, the poster symposium is an opportunity to talk to fellow researchers and their guests about the wide variety of projects students completed in ten weeks.

The event is open to the public with light refreshments served. Student researchers are also provided a lunch.

Preparing a Research Poster

  1. Work with a mentor to develop and implement a 10-week summer research project.
  2. Complete as much of the research as possible in a timely fashion.
  3. Write and submit an abstract to UGResearch@wsu.edu for the poster session.
  4. Attend our annual poster design workshop.
  5. Design a poster to be printed at the Large Format Printing Service Center. Remember that it takes time to print a poster, and you will not be the only person with a print job waiting in the print shop's queue. So be mindful of any deadlines!
  6. Pick up poster the day before the event.
  7. Get dressed up and come ready to talk about your research.

Poster-Making Resources

Download Poster templates and WSU Logos from the Large Format Print Center in the School of Mechanical and Materials Engineering.

Abstract Examples

Read abstracts from past poster sessions to get a better idea of how abstracts are written. A good abstract will provide a brief but interesting overview of what you uncovered through your research. It will impress your reader and make them want to know more about your work. If you want to learn more about how to write a strong abstract, you can consult the abstract writing tips availabile on the SURCA website.

Need Help With Your Poster?

For more information about the poster session or how to complete and abstract or poster design, please contact the Undergraduate Research Office at UGResearch@wsu.edu
